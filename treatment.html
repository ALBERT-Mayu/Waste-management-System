Biological Treatment

Biological treatment involves composting and anaerobic digestion, turning organic waste into valuable compost or biogas.

Guidelines:

  • Ensure organic waste is free from contaminants.
  • Maintain optimal moisture levels for effective composting.
  • Monitor anaerobic digesters for consistent biogas production.

Mechanical Treatment

Mechanical processes include shredding, compacting, and separating materials to prepare waste for further treatment or recycling.

Guidelines:

  • Inspect equipment regularly to prevent breakdowns.
  • Use advanced sorting technologies for improved efficiency.
  • Separate hazardous waste before processing.

Chemical Treatment

Chemical methods involve neutralizing or stabilizing hazardous waste using chemical agents, ensuring safe disposal.

Guidelines:

  • Conduct safety checks before handling chemicals.
  • Use appropriate protective equipment during treatment.
  • Store treated waste in compliant facilities.

Thermal Treatment

Thermal treatments, like incineration and pyrolysis, convert waste into energy or reduce it to inert ash.

Guidelines:

  • Maintain incinerator temperatures to ensure complete combustion.
  • Filter emissions to reduce environmental impact.
  • Monitor ash residue for potential reuse in construction materials.
